Examples of Stolen or Sampled Music

Because there is so much music out there, there are countless examples of possible cases of sampling and borrowing. Here we have some of the more obvious and popular examples.

"All Summer Long" Samples

This one is interesting. This sample is legal, and Kid Rock actually credits Bob Seger, Lynyrd Skynyrd, and Warren Zevon. The Wikipedia article about Kid Rock's version says this:""All Summer Long" is a song by American recording artist Kid Rock. It was released in 2008 as the third single from his seventh studio album, Rock n Roll Jesus. It was inspired by Bob Seger's song "Night Moves", and also samples "Sweet Home Alabama" by Lynyrd Skynyrd and "Werewolves of London" by Warren Zevon."

All Summer Long by Kid Rock, 2008

Werewolves of London, Warren Zevon, 1977

Night Moves, Bob Seger, 1976

Sweet Home Alabama, Lynyrd Skynyrd, 1973

"Princess" Sample

In 2007 Chris Brown recorded a song "Kiss Kiss" ft. T-Pain.

And in 2020 Pia Mia released a very similar song, "Princess" This song does also credit Chris Brown, so this is another legal sample.

"Show Goes On" Sample

In 2010, Lupe Fiasco released "Show Goes On." It contains a sample from a song by Modest House, "Float On"

In 2004, Modest House released "Float On"

"Surfin' USA" Sample

This instance almost led to a lawsuit. In 1963, The Beach Boys released Surfin’ USA. The song was, however, a copy of Chuck Berry’s Sweet Little Sixteen, with new lyrics written by Brian Wilson and Mike Love. There was no lawsuit because when the Beach Boys were approached about it, the manager of the band immediately gave the copyright to the song to Chuck Berry. They both receive credit and royalties for the song.

In 1957, Chuck Berry released his song, "Sweet Little Sixteen"

"Right Round" Sample

Released in 2009, "Right Round" even bears a similar name to the original, "You Spin Me Round." The original band is credited as songwriters.

In 1984, Dead or Alive released their song,"You Spin Me Round"
